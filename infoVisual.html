INFORMATION VISUALISATION

DESIGN BRIEF

I was challenged to produce an interactive visualisation and a story with text and other embedded static visualisations as a complete webpage.


MY SOLUTION

I chose to explore the relationship between tobacco consumption and tax. Using Spreadsheet, D3.js and Adobe Illustrator, I did data analysis and made a webpage, which contains an interactive map and diagram, as well as other visualisation graphs.


FOCUS: Interactive Information Visualisation

TOOLS: HTML/CSS, D3.js, Adobe Illustrator

DATE: Mar-Jun 2017

INTRODUCTION

AIM OF THE STORY

It is generally acknowledged that tobacco has detrimental effects on health. However, smoking remain prevalent these years all over the world. Governments of different countries has taken various measures to prevent tobacco consumption. Among all the effective preventions, tax levy is one of the most significant measures.

The aim of this story is to explore the effectiveness of the tax levy as a tobacco control measure. Specifically, the story maps the overall information on the global smoking rate from 2000 to 2025, compared with the tobacco tax in different country/regions, to reveal the relationship between the tobacco consumption and tax.


INTENDED AUDIENCE

The intend audience is the general public, including both smokers and non-smokers, taxpayers, as well as the policy makers. The story intends to provide with comprehensive information on the global smoking rate and its trend and forecast, then inform the audience about the effectiveness of tobacco tax in different countries.

As for smokers, they may want to know how much tax they pay for the cigarettes and the trend of smoking phenomenon. For the taxpayers, they have the rights to acquire information about every dollar they give for the tobacco control. Additionally, the policy makers can also have better understanding of the tax levy effectiveness by looking at data from other countries.


MAIN STRUCTURE OF THE STORY

This story begins by introducing the prevalence of tobacco consumption over the world, which is supported by a global map of smoking rate. This is an interactive map with information about the trend from 2000 to 2025. Then the tobacco tax in different countries/regions is introduced to give the audience a general view of the tax rate range. With that in mind, people may wonder the effectiveness of tax on tobacco control.

Therefore, a few countries with the highest tax are selected, compared with their smoking rate changes to reveal the correlation. In the end, Australia is taken as an example to show price elasticity of demand for tobacco products and the quit smoking trend.


Data Process and Analytics

GLOBAL SMOKING RATE

The original data set has been processed by deleting redundant information and integrating into an array. Only the data from year, country, sex and numeric are extracted.


TOBACCO PRICE AND TAX

The original data set has been split into six csv files, which represent six regions. For each single region, ‘international dollar’ is chosen as the price because this amount can best represent purchasing power parity. Similarly, ‘total tax’ is extracted as the tax figure. Then the average of price and tax ratio is calculated.


COMBINATION OF TAX AND SMOKING RATE DATA SET

This data set combines the previous two sets and selected the top 12 countries with the highest tobacco tax rate (over 80%). Since the tax rate is from 2014 data, two years of smoking rate are selected; one is before this tax rate (2010), the other is after this tax rate (2020 prediction). The smoking rate takes the average of male and female in the specific country that year.

At the end, the selected countries’ average and world’s average rates are calculated. Also the differences (smoking rate decrease) are found for comparison purpose later.
