Fish Mino

DESIGN BRIEF

We were challenged to create digital media content for Vivid Festival(2016) for DesignINK Ecology. The theme of this exhibition was marine conservation.


OUR SOLUTION

We, as a team of three design students, produced a short film and presented it to the public. The film tells a lifetime story of a fish named Mino, and reveals the relationship between human and ocean.


FOCUS: Video Production

TOOLS: Final Cut Pro, Adobe After Effects

DATE: Mar-Jun 2016

The Storyline

Human activities affect the ocean and the creatures that live in it in many different ways. We wanted to tell a deep and thoughtful story of the relations between human, ocean and environment. The video is based on a lifetime story of Fish Mino. At the beginning Mino family lived happily in the peaceful ocean. They enjoyed the time of playing with the starfish and shells in the corals. When human joins the sea, they had harmony at the first. However, human exploited underwater resources and occupy the ocean. This led to the destroy of marine life and human themselves.

Design Process

We were inspired by shadow puppetry, a Chinese ancient art form of movie and animation which uses flat articulated shapes to create body components of the figure, and held between a source of light and a translucent scrim. We made different models of marine lives and sticked with the wires to make them flexible and controllable in front of the green screen. And our hand interacted with the fish. Later, we turned the fish into silhouette by After Effects and replaced the background form navy blue, the mysterious nature of the sea, to red, where everything broke and disappeared.

Launch at Central Park

During the three months collaboration in the group of three, we promoted the ideas of ocean conservation by the Story of Fish Mino. The film was launched at a 15mx3m digital wall inside Central park of Sydney. Our film is under the theme "light, music and ideas" as part of designINK Ecology exhibition participated in Vivid Sydney 2016.
