HYPNOS

DESIGN BRIEF

We were challenged to design and prototype interactive experiences for use in homes that are based on Artificial Intelligence (AI). We focused on sleep problems and aimed to improve people’s sleep quality and help them to form good sleep habit.


SOLUTION

The final prototype - 'Hypnos' - is an AI driven holographic screen in the bedroom with voice control and leap motion. The main functions include viewing sleep pattern, setting sleep goals, forming sleep habit, relaxation and adjusting environment.


DESIGN TEAM

I collaborated with Yaohui and Zhendong on this projected. We all worked as UX/UI designer.

DESIGN PROCESS

In order to gain a comprehensive understanding of people’s needs, we did several rounds of user research through survey, interview, user diary, observation and focus group. We began to realize the underlying reasons why people couldn’t fall asleep, have bad sleep habits and poor quality and difficulty in getting up. Therefore, we divided our design into three main functions: form good sleep habit, improve sleep quality and motivate users to get up.


By testing our prototypes with the potential users, we got important insights and iterated our design several times. Our final prototype is a holographic screen, can be interacted with leap motion technology or voice control. The main functions include viewing sleep pattern, setting up smart goal, sleep aid, relaxation, and environment control.

Sleep Aid

AI will suggest the right amount of sleep you need every night and remind you to keep that goal. It understands your sleep stages, so he will only wake you up when you feel comfortable to do so.

Sleep Pattern

AI can retrieve data recorded by senses in the mattress, and analyse your sleep pattern.

Profile

Based on your sleep issues, the system can offer personalized tips and help you set up a sleep goal through a conversation with the AI.

Relaxation

With a magic mattress connected, you can do a massage session or meditation before sleep.

Environment

The system will create the best sleeping environment for you, subject to your adjustment. It will remember your preferences and will become smarter through machine learning.

Important Design Decisions

Combining 3 Initial Ideas

We evaluated the three prototypes and got similar total scores in Decision Matrix. We realised these three concepts are different aspects of the problem. Therefore we decided to combine them.


Creating a virtual character

Instead of interacting with a cold machine, we want our users to feel welcomed by talking with their personal sleep assistant. So the instructions and sleep settings are no longer dull, but become more adorable and friendly.


Holographic Screen in the Bedroom

Compared to mobile apps which requires the users’ initiative to pick up the phone, our holographic screen is much more convenient. It is always there, big and clear, ready for the user’s voice control.


Reflection

In the user research stage, we utilised a wide range of research methods, which provides us important insights and evidence throughout the design. When we got stuck between choices, we referred to our initial findings and focused on the users’ real needs and problems.

During iterative design stage, we did storyboard, sketch then from low fidelity to high fidelity prototype. Between each version of prototype, we did user test (following a pilot test) and refined the design according to the test results. This iterative process is necessary because it enabled us to improve the interface and interaction every time to achieve a better results.
